Our Story

Yaab Burgers takes something familiar – fried chicken – and builds it around the bold depth of East African berbere spice.

Berbere at the centre

Yaab started with a single question: how do you take the complexity of berbere and make it work inside a fast, modern burger kitchen without losing its soul?

Our in-house sauce is built on carefully measured batches of berbere spice, lemon, tomato, honey, black pepper, garlic and fats for richness. Every gram has a job. The result is a sauce that clings perfectly to crispy fried chicken and delivers heat, citrus, sweetness and spice in one hit.

Boneless chicken thighs are coated and cooked to order, dipped while hot, then stacked with charred slaw, pickles and berbere mayo inside a brioche bun. It’s comforting, messy in the right way and built to be craved.

Built to be consistent

Behind the scenes, Yaab is all about clean systems: standardised recipes, tight portioning, and a menu designed for speed and low waste. That makes the concept ideal for dark kitchens, delivery hubs or compact high-street units.

  • Authenticity – Respecting the heritage and complexity of berbere spice.
  • Precision – Batch recipes, QC checks and cook-to-order chicken.
  • Innovation – Not another southern-style copy; its own lane.
  • Memorability – A flavour profile customers recognise instantly.
